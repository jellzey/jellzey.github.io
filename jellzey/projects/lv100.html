LV-100

After several revisions of my lunchbox-reverb circuit, I ended up with a PCB design that I'm very pleased with. The new lunchverb, dubbed LV-100, has a re-voiced reverb driver and recovery stage, a new tone control, an input level switch, a 9V battery connection, and it has gained the ability to drive 600 ohm tanks as well as 8 ohm via an internal impedance switch.
By taking readings from a 1967 Super Reverb, I was able to get a clearer picture of what the Fender circuit is actually doing with the tank and surprisingly, I found way more low frequencies than I expected. By recreating that response and letting more low end through the driver with more high pass filtering in the recovery stage, I was able to get exactly the springy attack sound that I was after.

Here's a shot of some boards after being populated. The pots and jacks go on last to ensure a propper fit. The whole assembly comes together as a stack wusing M4 standoffs. The spacing of the tank's mounting holes is matched by every other layer. The faceplate is another PCB with solder pads for spring connecters to lock on to the lunchbox and give a strong ground connection. One of the biggest updates in this schematic is the elimination of the LM386. I found that by using a current source configuration, a much cheaper NE5532 can drive an 8 ohm tank just fine. A discrete transistor split rail buffer was added to replace the now preoccupied op-amp half. The new tone control is a basic op-amp LPF. Other than that, the design is functionally the same as the original A batch all ready to be housed in their lunchboxes. If theres any interest in PCB's, you can send me a message
